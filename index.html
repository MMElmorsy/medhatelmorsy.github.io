Medhat Elmorsy is currently a postdoctoral researcher at ETH Zurich, working at the intersection of earthquake engineering, structural dynamics, and digital construction. He holds a Doctor of Science degree from ETH Zurich (Switzerland), a Master degree in Civil Engineering (Major: structures) from the University of Alaska Anchorage (USA), a Bachelor degree in Civil Engineering from Mansoura University (Egypt).

Preparing to start my SNSF Postdoc.Mobility fellowship at MIT, focusing on seismic topology optimization and robotic fabrication of RC prototypes.

Our benchmark dataset from centrifuge shake table tests of 3D-printed URM structures has been accepted in Earthquake Spectra.

Two new papers on AM-based RC modeling and cyclic behavior of joints published in the Journal of Structural Engineering.
